Who Can Apply for Assistance?

Families who have lost their homes due to natural disasters
Individuals whose property was damaged by floods, fires, or storms
Communities affected by earthquakes or other catastrophic events
People experiencing financial hardship due to disaster-related job loss
Those needing help accessing government disaster relief programs
Anyone requiring temporary shelter or essential supplies

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I have to pay for your services?
No. All our disaster relief services are provided completely free of charge to survivors. We are funded entirely by donations and grants. You will never be asked to pay for assistance.
What documents do I need to apply?
You don't need any documents to submit this initial request. During the assessment process, we may ask for identification and proof of the disaster (photos, police reports, etc.), but we can help you obtain these if you don't have them.
How long can I stay in emergency housing?
Emergency housing is typically provided for 3-6 months, with possible extensions based on your recovery progress and needs. We work with you to transition to permanent housing solutions.
Can you guarantee I'll receive government disaster aid?
We cannot guarantee government aid approval as those decisions are made by government agencies. However, we significantly improve your chances by helping you complete applications correctly, gather proper documentation, and advocating on your behalf.
What if I'm not an American citizen?
Our emergency services are available to anyone affected by disaster in America, regardless of citizenship status. We help all people in need during crisis situations.
Can I apply if I already received some government help?
Yes! Government aid and our services can complement each other. We help fill gaps in support and provide services that government programs may not cover, such as mental health counseling or job training.
What if my situation changes after I submit the form?
Please contact your assigned case manager immediately if your situation worsens or changes. You can also call our hotline to update your information at any time.
Do you help with rebuilding permanent homes?
While we primarily provide temporary housing, our Community Rebuilding program can connect you with resources, grants, and partnerships that support permanent home reconstruction. We also help you access government rebuilding programs.
